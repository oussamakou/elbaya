# Talking to Guests — Farm El Baya

A short guide for anyone answering guest messages — Mahdi, Meriem, or anyone else helping out — across WhatsApp, Airbnb, Booking.com, or Lodgify. It's built entirely from real conversations that already worked well, plus a few real moments worth doing differently. Ready-made messages for common questions are in [LODGIFY_MESSAGES.md](LODGIFY_MESSAGES.md) — this guide is about the *voice* behind them.

## The voice, in short

- **Open warm, close warm.** "Bonjour [name], j'espère que vous allez bien 🙏" / "Hello [name], hope you're doing well :)" — nearly every good conversation starts this way. It costs nothing and it's genuinely the family's real style, not a script.
- **Say yes first, then work out the details.** A guest asks for a towel, some salt, a blanket, a strainer — the answer has always been an immediate yes, then sorting out the logistics. That instinct builds real goodwill; don't let it slip into hesitation.
- **Be proactively honest, even before it's asked.** Telling a guest about mosquitoes, or that three friendly dogs might visit, or that a nice restaurant is "the closest, but not amazing" — before they discover it themselves — is what makes the hospitality feel real instead of curated. Keep doing this.
- **Say "no" plainly when it's no.** "We don't currently offer a formal guided riding service." "Dinner isn't available tonight, but here's the kitchen." No over-explaining, no vague deflection. Guests trust plain honesty more than a maybe.
- **Keep it short.** The best replies in past chats are two or three sentences. A guest asking about Wi-Fi doesn't need a paragraph.
- **Match the guest's language and warmth.** French, English, Tunisian dialect, or a few words of the guest's own language when it fits (Meriem replying "Dankeschön" energy back to German guests, Mahdi's Tunisian expressions with guests who clearly enjoy it) — all fine, and it's part of what makes this feel like a real farm and not a hotel chain. Just keep the *substance* (prices, times, directions) in the fixed wording from the Quick Replies, so facts don't drift even when the tone flexes.
- **When something goes wrong on the guest's side (weather, missed flight, last-minute cancellation), make it easy on them.** "No worries, please stay safe" beats any attempt to salvage the booking. Guests remember how they were treated when something went wrong more than anything else.

## Two things worth doing differently

- **Quote prices from the Quick Replies, not from memory.** Breakfast alone has been quoted at four different real prices to different guests over time (20, 30, and 40 DT per couple, and 20 DT/person). None of this was dishonest — it's just what happens when a price is recalled rather than read. Copy-pasting the Quick Reply removes the risk entirely.
- **If you don't know something, say so and follow up — don't guess.** "Malheureusement je m'y connais pas" (about a hiking trail) is exactly right. A wrong answer costs more trust than a delayed one.

## The few facts that must never drift

However the conversation is going, these should always come out the same, because guests compare notes and because it's simply fair to everyone:

| Fact | Always say |
|---|---|
| Breakfast | 20 TND per person |
| Lunch / dinner | 40 TND per person, each |
| Check-in | From 2:00 PM |
| Check-out | By 11:00 AM |
| Deposit | 30%, refundable if cancelled 7+ days before arrival |
| Card payment | Not available on site — cash only for meals/extras |
| Directions | The GPS link *and* the blue-gate-then-left-between-the-olive-trees script — the link alone hasn't been enough for several guests |
| Contact | Mahdi for bookings/payments, Meriem for arrival and on-site questions — try to avoid handing out other numbers so it stays simple for guests |

If Mahdi changes a policy, update it in [LODGIFY_MESSAGES.md](LODGIFY_MESSAGES.md) so the Quick Replies stay the source of truth, rather than letting the new number live only in someone's memory.

## When it's not the Baya Room

Farm El Baya has three independent suites — **Baya** (the one on the direct website and Lodgify), **Elyssa**, and a third — mostly booked through Airbnb or Booking.com for the two that aren't on the direct site. If a conversation is about Elyssa or the other suite, double-check details before answering from habit — layout, kitchen access, and amenities can genuinely differ between suites.

## A quick reference for tone by situation

| Situation | Tone |
|---|---|
| First inquiry | Warm, welcoming, answer exactly what was asked — don't oversell |
| Day of arrival | Proactive check-in ("what time will you arrive?") + directions, sent before they have to ask |
| A request you can grant (early check-in, a towel, a later checkout) | Quick, warm yes |
| A request you can't grant | Plain, brief, no over-apologizing — offer the real alternative if there is one |
| A problem on the guest's side (bad weather, a wrong turn, a late flight) | Reassurance first, logistics second |
| A so-so review | Thank them for the honesty, don't get defensive, leave the door open for next time |
| A great review | Say what it actually meant to hear it — Mahdi and Meriem already do this well |
