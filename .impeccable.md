## Design Context

### Users
Domestic and international travelers considering a private farm stay near Testour, including couples, families, and active guests. They need to understand the room, trust the host, see what a stay can feel like, and request availability without navigating a hotel-style catalog.

### Brand Personality
Grounded, personal, and quietly premium. Farm El Baya should feel like a real working farm with thoughtful hospitality, not a resort, marketplace listing, or packaged attraction.

### Aesthetic Direction
A warm, light editorial experience led by honest photography, restrained olive accents, generous spacing, and a clear reading rhythm. Stay is concise and conversion-led; Experiences is immersive and story-led. Avoid repetitive card grids, dark promotional blocks, and dense price matrices.

### Design Principles
- Give each page one primary job and one obvious next action.
- Let photography and specific details create desire; use price lists only where guests need to compare.
- Reveal booking complexity progressively rather than presenting every option at once.
- Use unframed editorial layouts, rules, and whitespace before reaching for cards.
- Keep the farm's human, seasonal character visible in both copy and composition.
